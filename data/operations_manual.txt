## Section 1: Introduction and Purpose

### 1.1 Document Overview

This Internal Operations & Compliance Manual serves as the definitive guide for all 
ABC Bank employees regarding operational procedures, regulatory compliance, and 
internal policies. All staff members are required to familiarize themselves with 
the contents of this manual and adhere to the guidelines set forth herein.

### 1.2 Regulatory Framework

ABC Bank operates under the following regulatory frameworks:
- Federal Reserve Regulation E (Electronic Fund Transfers)
- Bank Secrecy Act (BSA) / Anti-Money Laundering (AML)
- Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act (GLBA) for customer privacy
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS)
- Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) sanctions

### 1.3 Document Updates

This manual is reviewed quarterly and updated as needed. All employees will be 
notified of significant changes via the internal communication system.

## Section 2: Account Operations

### 2.1 Account Opening Procedures

#### 2.1.1 Individual Accounts

Required documentation for individual account opening:
1. Government-issued photo ID (passport, driver's license, state ID)
2. Social Security Number verification
3. Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement dated within 90 days)
4. Initial deposit (minimum $25 for savings, $100 for checking)

Customer Identification Program (CIP) requirements:
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Residential address (P.O. boxes not accepted as primary)
- Identification number (SSN for U.S. citizens, passport number for non-citizens)

#### 2.1.2 Business Accounts

Required documentation for business account opening:
1. Articles of Incorporation or Organization
2. Certificate of Good Standing (for out-of-state entities)
3. Business license (if applicable)
4. EIN confirmation letter from IRS
5. Resolution authorizing account opening
6. Identification for all authorized signers

Beneficial ownership requirements (per FinCEN):
- Identify all individuals owning 25% or more of the entity
- Identify one individual with significant management responsibility

### 2.2 Account Maintenance

#### 2.2.1 Address Changes

Address changes may be processed through:
- In-branch with valid photo ID
- Online banking with security verification
- Phone banking with full account authentication

Processing timeline: Same business day if received before 3:00 PM local time.

#### 2.2.2 Account Closure

Standard account closure procedure:
1. Verify account holder identity
2. Check for pending transactions
3. Clear any outstanding fees
4. Obtain signed closure form
5. Issue remaining balance via check or transfer
6. Retain records per retention policy (7 years minimum)

## Section 3: Transaction Processing

### 3.1 Transaction Limits

#### 3.1.1 Daily Limits

| Transaction Type | Consumer Account | Business Account |
|------------------|------------------|------------------|
| ATM Withdrawal | $500 | $1,000 |
| Point of Sale | $2,500 | $5,000 |
| Online Transfer | $5,000 | $25,000 |
| Wire Transfer | $10,000 | $100,000 |

#### 3.1.2 Limit Increases

Temporary limit increases may be granted for:
- Pre-approved customers (credit score 720+)
- Accounts in good standing (12+ months, no overdrafts)
- Documented need (travel, large purchase)

Maximum temporary increase: 200% of standard limit for up to 30 days.

### 3.2 Wire Transfers

#### 3.2.1 Outgoing Wires

Processing requirements:
1. Verify customer identity (in-person or authenticated digital channel)
2. Collect all required beneficiary information
3. Screen against OFAC sanctions list
4. Obtain customer signature or digital authorization
5. Submit to wire room before 4:00 PM for same-day processing

Required information:
- Beneficiary name and address
- Beneficiary bank name, ABA routing number, and address
- Beneficiary account number
- Purpose of payment
- Amount in USD (or specify currency)

Fees: $30 domestic, $50 international

#### 3.2.2 Incoming Wires

Processing timeline:
- Domestic wires: Same business day
- International wires: 1-3 business days depending on correspondent bank

### 3.3 Check Processing

#### 3.3.1 Check Holds

Standard hold periods:
| Check Type | Amount | Hold Period |
|------------|--------|-------------|
| Local checks | $0-$225 | Next business day |
| Local checks | $226-$5,525 | 2 business days |
| Local checks | $5,526+ | 7 business days |
| Non-local checks | Any | Up to 7 business days |

Exceptions allowing extended holds:
- New accounts (under 30 days)
- Repeated overdrafts (6 or more in 6 months)
- Previously returned checks
- Emergency conditions

#### 3.3.2 Mobile Deposit

Mobile deposit limits:
- Standard accounts: $3,000/day, $10,000/month
- Premium accounts: $10,000/day, $50,000/month

Requirements for mobile deposit:
- Account must be open for 30+ days
- Customer must accept mobile deposit terms
- Check must be endorsed "For Mobile Deposit Only at ABC Bank"

## Section 4: Compliance Requirements

### 4.1 Anti-Money Laundering (AML)

#### 4.1.1 Suspicious Activity Monitoring

Red flags requiring enhanced review:
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile
- Rapid movement of funds with no apparent business purpose
- Unusual geographic patterns
- Structuring (multiple transactions designed to avoid reporting thresholds)
- Customer reluctance to provide information

#### 4.1.2 Currency Transaction Reports (CTR)

Filing requirements:
- Any cash transaction over $10,000 (single or aggregate)
- File within 15 calendar days
- Include all required customer and transaction information

#### 4.1.3 Suspicious Activity Reports (SAR)

Filing requirements:
- Suspected criminal activity involving $5,000 or more
- Suspected insider abuse involving $25,000 or more
- File within 30 calendar days of detection
- NO NOTIFICATION to customer of SAR filing

### 4.2 Know Your Customer (KYC)

#### 4.2.1 Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

EDD required for:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)
- High-risk geographic locations
- Cash-intensive businesses
- Private banking accounts over $1 million
- Correspondent accounts

EDD procedures:
1. Senior management approval required
2. Source of funds documentation
3. Enhanced transaction monitoring
4. Periodic reviews (minimum annually)

### 4.3 OFAC Compliance

#### 4.3.1 Sanctions Screening

Required screening points:
- Account opening
- Wire transfer initiation
- ACH origination
- Loan application
- Any customer-initiated international transaction

Potential match procedure:
1. Halt transaction immediately
2. Escalate to BSA/AML Officer
3. Document all findings
4. Block if confirmed match
5. File blocking report within 10 days

## Section 5: Customer Privacy

### 5.1 Privacy Notice Requirements

#### 5.1.1 Initial Notice

Provide initial privacy notice at account opening containing:
- Categories of information collected
- Categories of information disclosed
- Affiliates and third parties receiving information
- Security practices overview
- Opt-out instructions

#### 5.1.2 Annual Notice

Provide annual privacy notice to all active account holders by:
- December 31 each year, OR
- Within 12 months of initial notice

Delivery methods:
- Paper mail
- Electronic delivery (with customer consent)
- Included with periodic statement

### 5.2 Opt-Out Requirements

#### 5.2.1 Opt-Out Rights

Customers have the right to opt out of:
- Sharing with non-affiliated third parties
- Sharing for marketing purposes

Customers CANNOT opt out of:
- Sharing required by law
- Sharing to process transactions
- Sharing with service providers under contract

#### 5.2.2 Processing Opt-Out Requests

Opt-out requests must be processed within:
- 30 days of receipt
- Apply to all sharing immediately upon processing

Document all opt-out requests and maintain records for 5 years.

## Section 6: Information Security

### 6.1 Data Classification

#### 6.1.1 Classification Levels

| Classification | Description | Examples |
|----------------|-------------|----------|
| Public | Generally available | Marketing materials, branch addresses |
| Internal | For employees only | Policy documents, internal announcements |
| Confidential | Sensitive business info | Financial reports, strategic plans |
| Restricted | Highest sensitivity | Customer SSN, account numbers, passwords |

#### 6.1.2 Handling Requirements

Restricted data requirements:
- Encryption in transit (TLS 1.2+)
- Encryption at rest (AES-256)
- Access logging and monitoring
- Minimum necessary access principle
- Secure disposal (NIST 800-88 compliant)

### 6.2 Access Control

#### 6.2.1 User Access Management

Access request process:
1. Manager submits access request form
2. IT Security reviews against role requirements
3. Approval by system owner
4. Access provisioned within 3 business days
5. Confirmation sent to user and manager

Access review requirements:
- Monthly: Privileged access
- Quarterly: High-risk systems
- Annually: All system access

#### 6.2.2 Password Requirements

Password policy:
- Minimum 12 characters
- Must include: uppercase, lowercase, number, special character
- Cannot reuse last 12 passwords
- Maximum age: 90 days
- Lockout after 5 failed attempts

### 6.3 Incident Response

#### 6.3.1 Security Incident Classification

| Severity | Description | Response Time |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Critical | Data breach, system compromise | Immediate, 24/7 |
| High | Attempted breach, malware | Within 1 hour |
| Medium | Policy violation, suspicious activity | Within 4 hours |
| Low | Minor issues, informational | Within 24 hours |

#### 6.3.2 Reporting Procedures

All security incidents must be reported to:
- IT Security Hotline: x7777
- Email: security@abcbank.internal
- In person: Any IT Security team member

Required information:
- Date and time of discovery
- Systems or data affected
- Description of incident
- Any actions already taken

## Section 7: Customer Service Standards

### 7.1 Response Time Requirements

#### 7.1.1 Communication Channels

| Channel | Initial Response | Resolution |
|---------|------------------|------------|
| Phone | Answer within 60 seconds | Same call when possible |
| Email | Acknowledge within 4 hours | Within 24 hours |
| Secure Message | Acknowledge within 2 hours | Within 12 hours |
| In-Person | Immediate greeting | Minimize wait times |

#### 7.1.2 Escalation Paths

Escalation criteria:
- Customer request for supervisor
- Unresolved after 15 minutes
- Complaint about employee
- Legal threat
- Regulatory inquiry

Escalation timeline:
- Level 1 to Level 2: Within 5 minutes
- Level 2 to Manager: Within 15 minutes
- Manager to Executive: Same business day

### 7.2 Complaint Handling

#### 7.2.1 Complaint Documentation

All complaints must include:
- Customer name and account number
- Date and time of complaint
- Nature of complaint
- Actions taken
- Resolution outcome
- Customer satisfaction confirmation

#### 7.2.2 Regulatory Complaints

For complaints received from regulatory agencies:
1. Acknowledge receipt within 24 hours
2. Assign dedicated investigator
3. Complete investigation within 15 days
4. Provide detailed written response
5. Implement corrective actions as needed
6. Track for trend analysis
